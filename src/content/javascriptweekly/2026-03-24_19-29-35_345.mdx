[Announcing TypeScript 6.0](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/182654/d076523b1d "devblogs.microsoft.com") — Over six months in the making, TypeScript 6.0 is designed to bridge the gap between its self-hosted [compiler](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/182655/d076523b1d) and the (almost ready) [Go-powered native compiler of TypeScript 7.0](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/182656/d076523b1d) . There _are_ new features (Temporal improvements, `RegExp.escape`, and more), but most important are the changes to help you prepare for 7.0:

*   [Numerous default changes](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/182657/d076523b1d): `strict` is now `true`, `module` is `esnext`, `rootDir` defaults to `.`, and more.
*   A change that will affect many apps is `types` defaulting to `[]` rather than pulling in everything from `node_modules/@types`.
*   Numerous _deprecations_: the `es5` target, [emitting AMD, UMD, and SystemJS](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/182658/d076523b1d) modules, [`--baseUrl`](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/182659/d076523b1d), and others.
*   `--stableTypeOrdering` makes 6.0's type ordering behavior match 7.0's to help diagnose inference differences as you update.

Daniel Rosenwasser (Microsoft)
