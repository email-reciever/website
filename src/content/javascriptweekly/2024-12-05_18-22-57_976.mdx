There have been a few efforts to change this over the years but Oracle isn’t listening, so the Deno team formally filed a petition to cancel the trademark.

Deno

⚖️ And, as of this week, [it looks like Oracle is lawyering up to defend it **\[PDF\]**.](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/163112/d076523b1d)

🎂 [JavaScript Turns 29 Years Old](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/163115/d076523b1d "web.archive.org") — If you’re wondering _why_ Oracle has the JavaScript trademark, it's because Sun owned the name and [Oracle acquired Sun](https://javascriptweekly.com/link/163116/d076523b1d) (which is also why it owns Java). As someone who was posting inane questions in `comp.lang.javascript` in 1996, this news makes me feel old – no-one could have anticipated what JavaScript would be today.
