Draw in a field on the page (e.g. a signature or some handwriting), click a button, and get some [Motion](https://react.statuscode.com/link/183024/5d0bbb4a61) code you can drop into a component to re-animate what you drew. _(No, I can't draw to save my life, as evidenced above.. 😅)_

*   James Garbutt of the [e18e](https://react.statuscode.com/link/183026/5d0bbb4a61) project looks at [the three biggest causes of `node_modules` bloat](https://react.statuscode.com/link/183027/5d0bbb4a61): needless ES3-era compat packages, micro-libraries with a single consumer, and ponyfills for APIs that shipped years ago.
    
*   🤖 Phil Eaton surveyed 112 major source-available projects (Bun, Next.js, React, etc.) to [get a picture of their stance towards AI-assisted contributions](https://react.statuscode.com/link/183028/5d0bbb4a61). Of those, only four have an outright ban.
    
*   🔒 More than ever, it's essential to ensure no secrets have snuck into your repos. [Secretlint](https://react.statuscode.com/link/183029/5d0bbb4a61) is a linting tool dedicated entirely to the task. It's written in Node but can be used with Docker against projects of any language.
