[A New Chapter for Express.js](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164218/0a4070b383 "expressjs.com") — 2024 saw the still-extremely-popular [Express](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164219/0a4070b383) project awaken from a slumber, of sorts, with work being made to update things to modern standards, [a security audit](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164220/0a4070b383), and the [release of Express v5.](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164221/0a4070b383) Here, the team explains what’s been going on behind the scenes to get Express back on the tracks, as well as a **“bold vision for 2025.”**

Express Technical Committee

[![](https://res.cloudinary.com/cpress/image/upload/c_limit,w_480,h_480,q_auto/copm/0b57f4f6.png)](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164217/0a4070b383)

[How to Create Your Own AI Agent](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164217/0a4070b383 "frontendmasters.com") — Join Scott Moss for this video course exploring how to build an AI agent from scratch — covering LLM options, transformers, token limits, AI memory, function calling, agent loops, prompting, and much more.

Frontend Masters sponsor

[Node v23.6.0 (Current) Released](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164222/0a4070b383 "nodejs.org") — In last week’s issue, we said Node 23.6 was imminent, and it came out a few hours later ;-) Along with the usual dependency updates and bug fixes, the big news is type stripping is now enabled by default, enabling that `node yourapp.ts` magic, described in more detail below..

Marco Ippolito

[Node’s New Built-in Support for TypeScript Explained](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164223/0a4070b383 "2ality.com") — [Node.js v23.6.0 (Current)](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164222/0a4070b383) (above) makes Node’s new type-stripping features work by default. Dr. Axel digs into how it works and what the limitations are. A handy primer.

Dr. Axel Rauschmayer

**IN BRIEF:**

*   Ecma International has a new technical committee: [TC55 - Web Interoperable Server Runtimes.](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164224/0a4070b383) It takes over from the former WinterCG group as a forum for working on ways for server-side JS runtimes (e.g. Node, Deno, Bun) to better interoperate and align their APIs.
    
*   Popular Node.js-powered static site generator [Eleventy (11ty) takes a look back at what happened in 2024.](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164225/0a4070b383)
    
*   [Node v22.13.0 (LTS)](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164226/0a4070b383) was released with the [permission model](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164227/0a4070b383) becoming stable.
    
*   The Electron project is [moving its ecosystem over to Node 22.](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164228/0a4070b383)
    

[Horizontal, Vertical, Diagonal? What Even Is Scaling?! 👀](https://nodeweekly.com/link/164229/0a4070b383 "judoscale.com") — Up, down, left, right… A+B, A+B?? No no.. scaling isn’t that complicated. Let’s unpack horizontal vs. vertical scaling.
